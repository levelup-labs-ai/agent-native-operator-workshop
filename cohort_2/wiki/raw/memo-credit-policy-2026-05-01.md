# Credit policy memo

Date: May 1, 2026
Authors: Diego, Maya
Audience: Foxglove team

## Decision

Foxglove now has a formal credit policy for customer-impacting outages and onboarding delays. Replaces the ad-hoc credit Diego applied for Brightline on Apr 22.

## Why now

The Brightline integration outage (Apr 22) was the first real test of how we handle customer-facing delivery failures. Brightline is on Starter at 8 seats ($2,000/mo). Diego improvised a 2-month credit ($4,000) to keep the customer in the seat while Tomás shipped the fix. That worked, but we shouldn't be improvising the next time.

## The policy

### Tier 1: Service outage (the product is down or broken for the customer)

- Up to 4 hours: no credit. Acknowledge in writing. Postmortem within 5 business days.
- 4 to 24 hours: 1 week credit on monthly fee.
- 1 to 5 days: 1 month credit. Postmortem to customer within 2 business days. CS lead (Diego) calls the POC.
- 5+ days: 2 month credit. Mandatory exec involvement (Maya). Credit applied automatically; customer does not need to ask.

### Tier 2: Onboarding delay (customer can't get to value because of us)

- Up to 1 week beyond promised go-live: no credit. Communication only.
- 1 to 3 weeks: 1 month credit, applied to first invoice.
- 3+ weeks: 2 month credit. Maya escalation.

### Tier 3: Feature failure (a specific capability isn't working as sold)

- Case by case. Diego decides up to 1 month credit. Above that, Maya signs off.

## What this changes for Brightline

The 2 month credit Diego applied Apr 22 is consistent with the new Tier 1 (5+ days) policy. Their integration was down Apr 22 to Apr 30, exactly the threshold. Locking that in. No clawback.
